# Session: 2026-04-09 — Forecast Engine & Predictive Models

## What Shipped
- **Demand Forecasting Engine** (`src/data/forecastEngine.js`)
  - Built from 8,047 daily demand records (Apr 2023 – Apr 2026) across 10 states
  - Territory × day-of-week historical averages (e.g., NYE peaks at ~9.1 Mon/Wed/Fri)
  - Monthly seasonality indices: Jul-Aug are 1.23–1.28× average, Dec is 0.70×
  - 90-day trend tracking per territory (CT +4.8% growing, MARI -38.2% declining)
  - Staffing calculator: demand × sit rate ÷ 3 slots/rep = reps needed
  - Time-slot demand scoring (11:30 AM highest at 24%, 7 PM lowest at 12%)

- **Sit Rate Prediction Model**
  - Built from 112,656 appointment observations across 37 months
  - 7 lead-source categories with sit rates:
    - Get the Referral: **46.9%** (best)
    - Inbound/Website: **35.8%**
    - Paid leads: **30.3%** (largest volume: 79K observations)
    - Partners: **22.1%**
    - Self Gen/Canvass: **18.6%**
    - Retail (BJs, Ace): **17.3%**
  - Monthly trend tracking (currently 30.8%, stable with slight improvement from 28.4% a year ago)

- **ForecastPanel UI Component** (`src/components/ForecastPanel.jsx`)
  - 3-tab sidebar: Demand Forecast | Sit Rate | Staffing
  - Demand tab: territory breakdown with confidence intervals, week sparkline, seasonal context
  - Sit Rate tab: category bars, 6-month trend, monthly mini chart
  - Staffing tab: reps needed per territory, time-slot demand scores

- **Data-Driven Insights** — Smart Insights sidebar now pulls live from forecast engine
  - Peak demand alerts, sit rate trends, seasonal context, territory decline warnings

## Architecture Decision
- Forecast engine is a pure JS module with no runtime dependencies — all model weights are embedded as constants derived from the Salesforce data analysis
- No ML library needed; the model uses weighted historical averages with seasonality and trend adjustments, which matches the data well given 3 years of history
- Confidence intervals set at ±30% for single-day forecasts (appropriate for this sample size)
